## 1. System Classification

**System Type:** Conversational Assistant with Tool Use (Hybrid — forecasting + anomaly detection + NLQ)

**Description:**
A Python-based conversational AI service that connects to the Zoho Creator Budget Tracking & Inventory Management system via REST APIs. It provides project managers, finance managers, and procurement teams with natural language querying over financial data, automated budget burn forecasts (30/60/90-day), anomaly detection in spending patterns, and smart procurement recommendations. "Good" looks like: a project manager asks "Which project is at risk of exceeding budget?" and gets a ranked answer with evidence, or an automated weekly summary email that surface critical budget health indicators without manual effort.

**Critical Failure Modes:**
1. **Financial hallucination** — AI reports incorrect budget figures, leading managers to make wrong spending decisions
2. **Missed overrun detection** — Anomaly detection fails to flag a genuine budget overrun, causing unapproved overspend
3. **Procurement recommendation error** — Recommends reorder of wrong quantity or timing, causing stockout or overstock
4. **Data staleness** — AI acts on stale Zoho Creator data, producing forecasts disconnected from current reality
5. **False anomaly flag** — Frequent false positives erode user trust, leading to alerts being ignored

## 1b. Domain Context

> Researched by `gsd-domain-researcher`. Grounds the evaluation strategy in domain expert knowledge.

**Industry Vertical:** Construction Project Management — Financial Operations (India)

**User Population:** Project Managers (budget tracking, milestone spend approvals), Finance Managers (forecast validation, GST compliance oversight), Procurement Team (reorder decisions, vendor payment scheduling), Inventory Managers (stock level monitoring, reorder point alerts), Compliance Officers (GST return filing, audit trail maintenance, HSN code verification) — internal ITOTCloud delivery team and client project stakeholders

**Stakes Level:** High — financial decisions (spending approvals, procurement orders, GST compliance, milestone payments) are informed by AI outputs. Errors can cascade into regulatory penalties, project delays, and contractual disputes.

**Output Consequence:** Budget forecasts may trigger spending approvals; procurement recommendations may create purchase orders; anomaly flags may initiate audit investigations; GST/HSN suggestions influence tax return filings. Incorrect outputs lead to real financial impact including tax penalties (up to 100% of tax evaded under CGST Act), stockout-induced project delays costing ₹1L+/day, or overspend that erodes project margins by 5-15%.

### What Domain Experts Evaluate Against

| Dimension | Good (expert accepts) | Bad (expert flags) | Stakes | Source |
|-----------|----------------------|-------------------|--------|--------|
| Budget Accuracy | Forecast within ±10% of actual spend over 30-day window; e.g., ₹50L project at 55% spend mid-year | Forecast off by >20%; e.g., predicts ₹5L overrun but actual is ₹12L | High — wrong budget decisions | Finance Manager rubric |
| Anomaly Precision | Flags genuine overruns; <5% false positive rate; e.g., ₹3L material spike on a ₹10L component | Frequent false flags that waste PM time; e.g., flags routine monthly vendor payment as anomaly | Medium — trust erosion | Project Manager rubric |
| Forecast Horizon | 30/60/90-day projections with confidence intervals; e.g., "Next 30d burn: ₹4.2L ±8%" | Projections without uncertainty bounds; e.g., "Will spend ₹4.2L next month" with no error margin | High — planning depends on it | Finance Manager rubric |
| Procurement Timing | Reorder timing aligns with lead time + reorder level; e.g., steel reorder triggered 2 weeks before stockout | Recommends too early (excess stock tying ₹2L capital) or too late (site停工 due to stockout) | High — inventory impact | Inventory Manager rubric |
| GST/HSN Code Accuracy | Correctly maps expenses to GST rates; e.g., 18% on construction services, 5% on cement | Misclassifies HSN codes — applies 12% GST on nil-rated items, causing incorrect ITC claims | High — tax compliance & penalty risk | Compliance Officer rubric |
| Material vs Labor Classification | Correctly separates material costs (₹8L) from labor costs (₹2L) in project expense reports | Merges contractor labor with material line items, skewing component-level forecasts | Medium — component forecast error | Finance Manager rubric |

| Answer Correctness | NLQ responses cite specific project names, amounts in ₹, and dates; e.g., "Project Pearl spent ₹28L of ₹40L (70%)" | Vague or fabricated numbers; e.g., says ₹35L spent when actual is ₹28L | Critical — financial decisions | PM + Finance rubric |

### Known Failure Modes in This Domain

1. **Lookback bias** — Forecasts extrapolate from recent anomalous months (e.g., a large one-time material purchase) as if they are baseline
2. **Seasonality blindness** — Budget patterns that repeat quarterly (e.g., hardware procurement cycles, contractor billing cycles) are not accounted for
3. **Orphaned data noise** — Zoho Creator lookup fields may reference stale inventory/budget data, skewing predictions
4. **Category confusion** — AI misclassifies expense types (e.g., materials vs. services vs. labor) leading to wrong component-level forecasts
5. **Granularity mismatch** — User asks about monthly budget but AI replies with project-to-date aggregate, causing confusion
6. **GST/HSN misclassification** — AI assigns wrong GST rate or HSN code to an expense category, leading to incorrect tax reporting and potential ITC claim rejection
7. **Contractor payment duplication** — Same invoice recorded in both project expenses and vendor procurement modules, inflating actual spend figures
8. **Material price escalation blindness** — Forecast does not account for known/announced price hikes in construction materials (steel, cement), producing optimistic projections
9. **Budget head miscoding** — Expense posted to wrong WBS element (e.g., foundation costs booked under finishing), corrupting component-level analysis
10. **Retention money confusion** — Withheld contract retention (typically 10%) is treated as spent, overstating utilization percentage
11. **Milestone timing mismatch** — AI assumes linear monthly spend but construction projects follow milestone-based billing (e.g., 30% at foundation, 40% at structure), causing skewed forecasts

### Regulatory / Compliance Context

Operating in India with INR transactions, this system interacts with several regulatory frameworks:

- **GST Act, 2017** — Every expense and procurement transaction involves GST (CGST + SGST or IGST). The AI must not misinterpret or misreport tax components. Incorrect HSN code mapping or GST rate application could lead to incorrect Input Tax Credit (ITC) claims, attracting penalties under Section 73/74 of the CGST Act.
- **Income Tax Act, 1961 — Record Keeping** — Section 44AA read with Rule 6F requires maintenance of books of account for at least 6 years. The AI's outputs (forecasts, anomaly reports) may form part of audit trail documentation. Accuracy standards must match record-keeping obligations.
- **Companies Act, 2013 — Financial Records** — If projects are run through an entity covered under the Act, Section 128 requires proper bookkeeping and an audit trail. AI-generated reports used in management reviews must be traceable to source data.
- **Invoice & E-invoicing Rules** — For B2B transactions above ₹5L, e-invoicing under GST Rules may apply. AI-generated procurement recommendations must not suggest purchase orders that violate e-invoicing thresholds or reporting requirements.
- **RERA (Real Estate Regulation Act) 2016** — If projects involve real estate construction, RERA mandates separate bank accounts for each project and quarterly progress reporting. Budget forecasts must align with RERA-mandated project timelines.
- **Data Localization (RBI / IT Act)** — All financial data resides in India; no cross-border transfer of budget/expense records is permitted. The AI service must run on India-hosted infrastructure.

The system itself is exempt from direct regulation as an internal management tool, but the data it processes carries statutory obligations. AI outputs that inform financial decisions or client reports must meet audit-quality accuracy — errors could cascade into regulatory non-compliance by the user.

## 2. Framework Decision

**Selected Framework:** LangChain

**Version:** v0.3.x (Python)

**Rationale:**
LangChain resolves the OpenAI-vs-open-source tension by providing a model-agnostic abstraction layer — prototype fast with OpenAI GPT-4o today, swap to self-hosted Llama/Mistral via Ollama or vLLM with a config change tomorrow. It's open-source, Python-native, has the largest community for solo dev support, and its conversational memory + tool-use primitives directly map to the NLQ + budget summary + anomaly detection requirements. The 25% dev-speed boost over scratch gets a working demo fastest for the solo dev stage.

**Alternatives Considered:**

| Framework | Ruled Out Because |
|-----------|------------------|
| LlamaIndex | Better for pure RAG/document Q&A; the primary need here is conversational agent with tool use, not document retrieval. Kept as fallback if NLQ over financial documents becomes dominant. |
| OpenAI Agents SDK | Eliminated by constraints: closed-source, vendor lock-in, no self-hosted option |
| Claude Agent SDK | Eliminated by constraints: closed-source, Claude-only, no self-hosted option |
| CrewAI | Eliminated: optimized for multi-agent orchestration, not single-agent conversational assistant |
| Haystack | Eliminated: NLP pipeline focus lacks the conversational agent primitives needed here |

**Vendor Lock-In Accepted:** Partial — initial OpenAI model dependency is accepted for speed of prototyping, mitigated by LangChain's abstraction layer enabling model swap later

### Key Abstractions
| Concept | What It Is | When You Use It |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| Tool / StructuredTool | Function decorated with `@tool` or `StructuredTool.from_function` | Wrapping Zoho Creator API calls with typed schemas (budget, expenses, inventory) |
| create_tool_calling_agent | Factory that wires LLM + tools + prompt into an agent | Setting up the main conversational agent (replaces deprecated `create_openai_tools_agent`) |
| AgentExecutor | Runtime that orchestrates LLM reasoning + tool call loop | Running the conversational loop for each user query |
| ChatPromptTemplate | Structured prompt with system/human/message placeholders | Defining the agent's persona, rules, tool-use instructions, and budget context |
| MessagesPlaceholder | Dynamic slot for chat history or agent scratchpad in prompt | Injecting multi-turn context (`chat_history`) or intermediate reasoning (`agent_scratchpad`) |
| ConversationBufferMemory | Stores chat history for context-aware follow-ups | Multi-turn conversations about budgets and forecasts (prototype); migrate to LangGraph checkpointing for production |
| RunnableConfig | Per-invocation configuration (thread_id, callbacks, metadata) | Tracing individual queries in Langfuse, setting per-request tags |
| with_structured_output | Chains a Pydantic schema to an LLM call for typed output | Extracting structured BudgetHealthReport or AnomalyFlag from natural language |
| trim_messages | Sliding-window message truncation within context limits | Keeping conversation + budget data under the token budget during long sessions |
| BaseMessage (SystemMessage, HumanMessage, AIMessage) | Typed message objects LangChain uses internally | Building prompts programmatically, inspecting agent reasoning traces |

### Common Pitfalls
1. **Tool timeout** — Zoho Creator API calls may be slow; set generous timeouts on tool calls (30s+) and handle failures gracefully
2. **Token overflow** — Budget data with many line items exceeds context; paginate tool results and summarize before returning to LLM
3. **Circular reasoning** — Agent may call the same tool repeatedly; limit retries via `max_iterations` (recommend 5) in AgentExecutor and use `early_stopping_method="generate"`
4. **Date ambiguity** — User says "this month" but system needs explicit dates; normalize relative dates to absolute in the prompt
5. **Deprecated `create_openai_tools_agent`** — In v0.3.x this was removed; use `create_tool_calling_agent` instead. The old import silently fails or raises `ImportError`.
6. **Tool description too vague** — LangChain's agent uses tool descriptions to decide which tool to call. A tool named `get_data` with docstring "gets data" causes misrouting. Always write explicit descriptions: `"""Fetch budget vs actual for a project from Zoho Creator. Returns utilization percentage and component breakdown."""`
7. **`@tool` signature mismatch** — The `@tool` decorator inspects function type hints. If you annotate `project_id: str` but pass an int, LangChain fails to serialize the tool call. Always match types between tool signatures and what Zoho Creator expects.
8. **Stale conversation buffer** — `ConversationBufferMemory` grows unbounded. After ~10 turns, token usage spikes. Use `trim_messages` or switch to LangGraph's checkpointing for production.
9. **Tool result format inconsistency** — If `get_budget_utilization` returns a dict but all other tools return strings, the LLM struggles to parse results. Normalize all tool outputs to a consistent format (preferably structured dicts or markdown tables).
10. **Binding tools at invoke-time instead of init-time** — Tools must be passed to `create_tool_calling_agent` at construction. Passing `tools` only to `AgentExecutor` (and not to the agent factory) causes a silent no-op where the agent has no tools available.

## 4. Implementation Guidance

**Model Configuration:**
```python
llm = ChatOpenAI(
    model="gpt-4o",
    temperature=0,          # Deterministic for financial queries
    max_tokens=2048,
    timeout=60,
    max_retries=2,
)
```
Use `gpt-4o-mini` for simple queries (cost optimization) and `gpt-4o` for complex forecasting/anomaly analysis. Temperature 0 for all financial queries to minimize hallucination risk.

**Core Pattern:**
Tool Calling Agent pattern via `create_tool_calling_agent` (replaces deprecated `create_openai_tools_agent` from v0.2.x). The LLM uses function calling to fetch data from Zoho Creator via REST API tools, then reasons over the results. For scheduled tasks (weekly summaries), use LangGraph for the workflow with conditional branching based on alert thresholds.

**Tool Configuration Pattern:**
```python
from langchain.tools import StructuredTool

def get_project_expenses_impl(project_id: str, start_date: str, end_date: str) -> dict:
    """Internal implementation — calls zoho_client.get_expenses()."""
    # Add caching, error handling, rate limiting here
    ...

get_project_expenses = StructuredTool.from_function(
    func=get_project_expenses_impl,
    name="get_project_expenses",
    description="Fetch expense records for a project within a date range (ISO format YYYY-MM-DD). Returns aggregated totals and component breakdown.",
    handle_tool_error=True,  # LangChain catches exceptions and returns error message to LLM
)
```

Use `StructuredTool.from_function` when you need explicit schema control for Zoho Creator API parameters. The `handle_tool_error=True` flag prevents tool crashes from breaking the agent loop — the error message is returned to the LLM so it can retry or apologize.

**Tool Inventory:**
| Tool | Input | Output | Caching |
|------|-------|--------|---------|
| `get_project_budget` | project_id | Budget plan + component breakdown (aggregated) | 5 min |
| `get_project_expenses` | project_id, start_date, end_date | Expense records with totals | 5 min |
| `get_inventory_status` | item_id | Current stock level, reorder point | 5 min |
| `get_procurement_history` | item_id, months | PO history for consumption forecasting | 5 min |
| `list_projects` | optional filter string | Matching project IDs and names for NLQ disambiguation | 1 min (freshness matters) |

All tools must:
- **Cache** Zoho Creator API responses (5 min TTL, 1 min for project listing) using `functools.lru_cache` or a dedicated cache layer
- **Aggregate** results before returning to LLM — never expose raw line items
- **Handle errors** gracefully — wrap API calls in try/except and return user-friendly messages
- **Normalize outputs** to consistent format (dict with `success: bool, data: Any, error: Optional[str]`)

**State Management:**
Conversation history via `ConversationBufferMemory` (in-memory for prototype). For production, migrate to LangGraph's checkpointing with SQLite/Postgres persistence — this gives you time-travel debugging, human-in-the-loop, and durable state across restarts. Scheduled workflow state (weekly summary runs, forecast recalculation) stored in a local SQLite database via LangGraph's `MemorySaver`:

```python
from langgraph.checkpoint.memory import MemorySaver
from langgraph.graph import StateGraph

checkpointer = MemorySaver()
# Pass to LangGraph's StateGraph for auto-checkpointing on every node transition
```

For the prototype phase, `ConversationBufferMemory` with `trim_messages` is acceptable keep the scope small.

**Context Window Strategy:**
Summarize budget/expense data before passing to LLM — never dump raw line items. Use a tiered approach:
1. Top-level aggregates (total budget, total spent, utilization %)
2. Component-level breakdown (top 5 by variance)
3. Drill-down to individual records only when user explicitly asks

### Structured Outputs with Pydantic

```python
from pydantic import BaseModel, Field
from typing import Optional
from datetime import datetime

class BudgetHealthReport(BaseModel):
    project_name: str = Field(description="Name of the project")
    total_budget: float = Field(description="Total approved budget amount")
    total_spent: float = Field(description="Total spent to date")
    utilization_pct: float = Field(description="Budget utilization percentage")
    days_remaining: Optional[int] = Field(description="Days until project end date", default=None)
    status: str = Field(description="One of: on_track, at_risk, over_budget")
    forecast_30d_burn: float = Field(description="Predicted spend in next 30 days")
    confidence_low: float = Field(description="Lower bound of 80% confidence interval")
    confidence_high: float = Field(description="Upper bound of 80% confidence interval")

class AnomalyFlag(BaseModel):
    project_id: str
    component: str = Field(description="Budget component name")
    expected_range: str = Field(description="Expected monthly range")
    actual_value: float
    severity: str = Field(description="low, medium, high")
    recommendation: str = Field(description="Suggested action")
```

Use `with_structured_output()` for extraction and output generation — this is LangChain's PydanticOutputParser replacement in v0.3.x:

```python
from pydantic import BaseModel, Field
from langchain_openai import ChatOpenAI

class BudgetAlert(BaseModel):
    project_name: str = Field(description="Project name from Zoho Creator")
    severity: str = Field(description="One of: info, warning, critical")
    message: str = Field(description="Human-readable alert message")
    suggested_action: str = Field(description="What the PM should do")

llm = ChatOpenAI(model="gpt-4o-mini", temperature=0)
structured_llm = llm.with_structured_output(BudgetAlert)

# Use in agent tools or standalone extraction
result = structured_llm.invoke("Project Sapphire has spent 85% of budget with 2 months left")
# result is a BudgetAlert instance, not raw JSON
assert isinstance(result, BudgetAlert)
```

Key rules for `with_structured_output`:
- Always set `temperature=0` for deterministic schema output
- Use `gpt-4o-mini` for simple extraction (budget health calls), `gpt-4o` for complex multi-field reports
- Do NOT pass `response_format={"type": "json_object"}` in model kwargs — `with_structured_output` handles this internally
- Always validate Pydantic models before returning to user (`.model_dump()` for serialization)

### Async-First Design

Use `async` for all Zoho Creator API calls to avoid blocking the agent loop. The one common mistake: forgetting to `await` tool results — LangChain's AgentExecutor handles async natively. Stream responses for NLQ queries; await full response for scheduled tasks.

### Prompt Engineering Discipline

Separate system prompt (persona + rules) from user prompt (query). Use few-shot examples for complex queries (budget comparisons, trend analysis). Token budget: reserve 2000 tokens for system prompt + examples, 4000 for budget data, rest for conversation.

**Budget-specific system prompt template:**
```
You are a Budget Intelligence assistant for ITOTCloud's construction project management system.
You have access to Zoho Creator data via the available tools.

RULES:
- NEVER fabricate budget figures. If a tool returns no data, say "No data available."
- Always cite specific project names, amounts (INR), and dates in your answers.
- When comparing budgets, use percentages AND absolute values.
- For forecasts, always state the confidence interval alongside the point estimate.
- If you detect a possible anomaly, flag it with the component name and expected range.
- For inventory queries, include reorder point and current stock in your answer.
- When you are unsure about a date range, ask the user to clarify rather than assuming.
```

**Financial query-specific instructions to anchor in the prompt:**
- "Express all currency amounts in INR with proper formatting (₹XX,XXX)."
- "When showing budget utilization, always include: total budget, total spent, remaining, and percentage."
- "For project comparisons, rank by risk level: over_budget > at_risk > on_track."
- "If asked about a project by name, use list_projects to resolve the project_id first."
- "Do not perform arithmetic on budget figures — trust the tool outputs."

Include explicit instruction: "If you cannot find the exact data, say so — do not fabricate numbers."

Use the `hub.pull` pattern from LangChain Hub for prompt versioning:
```python
from langchain import hub
prompt = hub.pull("itotcloud/budget-agent-v1")
# Version-controlled, inspectable, shareable across the team
```

### Context Window Management

For conversational agent: summarize after 5 turns using a background summarization chain. For budget data: always summarize aggregates, never pass raw line items. Use LangChain's `trim_messages` to keep recent turns and drop oldest when approaching limit.

### Cost and Latency Budget

Per-query cost estimate: ~$0.02-0.05 for simple NLQ (gpt-4o-mini), ~$0.10-0.30 for complex forecasting (gpt-4o). Cache frequent queries (common budget checks). Route simple classification (e.g., "is this a forecast or a lookup?") to gpt-4o-mini first, escalate to gpt-4o only when needed.

### LLM Judge Calibration

LLM judges (GPT-4o evaluating GPT-4o-mini outputs) are used for subjective dimensions: Anomaly Precision, NLQ Correctness, GST/HSN Code Accuracy, Budget Transfer Tracking, and Summary Usefulness. Before trusting LLM judge scores, calibrate against human domain expert judgment:

**Calibration Protocol:**
1. **Initial calibration round** — Domain experts (Finance Manager, Project Manager, Compliance Officer) score 20 representative examples spanning all dimensions
2. **Judge scoring** — LLM judge scores the same 20 examples using the domain-anchored rubric
3. **Correlation check** — Compute Cohen's Kappa or Spearman correlation between human and LLM judge scores
4. **Acceptance threshold** — Target κ ≥ 0.7 before relying on LLM judge scores in automated eval pipelines
5. **Recalibration cadence** — Re-run calibration quarterly or when system prompt/tools change materially
6. **Explicit disagreement review** — Any LLM judge score that diverges >1 point from human baseline is flagged for manual review and added to a disagreement tracking log

**What to calibrate against:**
- Finance Manager rubric for Budget Accuracy and Anomaly Precision
- Compliance Officer rubric for GST/HSN Code Accuracy and ITC eligibility
- Project Manager rubric for NLQ Correctness and Summary Usefulness

**Fallback:** If LLM judge correlation falls below 0.7 after recalibration, revert to 100% human review for the affected dimension until the judge prompt or rubric is updated.

## 7. Production Monitoring

**Tracing Tool:** Langfuse (open-source, self-hosted on India-hosted infrastructure — AWS Mumbai or equivalent)

**Key Metrics to Track:**
1. Average response time per query type (NLQ vs forecast vs anomaly check vs GST/regulatory query)
2. Token usage per query (cost tracking, segmented by model: gpt-4o vs gpt-4o-mini)
3. Tool call success rate (Zoho Creator API availability — tracked per endpoint)
4. User feedback score (thumbs up/down ratio, segmented by query type)
5. Forecast accuracy vs actual (weekly rollup, segmented by milestone-billed vs linear projects)
6. **GST/HSN code accuracy score** — LLM judge score on sampled GST-related outputs (weekly)
7. **Regulatory compliance flag rate** — count of online guardrail interventions triggered per day (GST hallucination, retention double-count, milestone mismatch, cross-border data, e-invoice threshold)
8. **AI output audit trail completeness** — % of outputs that include required fields (project name, INR amount, date, disclaimer) per Section 1b Regulatory Context (IT Act Section 44AA record-keeping)
9. **Retention money error rate** — % of cash flow forecasts that incorrectly treat withheld retention as spent (weekly)
10. **LLM judge / human score divergence** — count of evaluations where LLM judge score deviates >1 point from human reviewer score (weekly), tracked per dimension

**Alert Thresholds:**
- Tool call failure rate > 10% in 5-minute window → page (Zoho API may be down)
- Average response time > 30s → investigate (query complexity or model issue)
- Forecast error > 20% for 2 consecutive weeks → review data pipeline and milestone schedule alignment
- Any negative financial figure hallucination detected → immediate human review
- **GST/HSN misclassification rate > 5% in any week** → alert Compliance Officer, block deployment of new prompts until resolved. Rationale: CGST Act Section 73/74 penalty exposure.
- **Retention money error detected in any cash flow forecast** → alert Finance Manager for manual verification. Rationale: directly misrepresents available budget.
- **Regulatory compliance flag rate > 10 per day** → investigate root cause, may indicate model drift or data pipeline issue
- **LLM judge / human score divergence > 1 point for >20% of evaluations in a week** → recalibrate judge prompt (see Section 5 Calibration Protocol)
- **E-invoice threshold violation flag triggered** → alert Procurement Lead to ensure e-invoice is generated
- **Cross-border data detection flag triggered** → immediate security incident response (data localization violation)

**Smart Sampling Strategy:**

Sampling is weighted toward high-signal interactions — those with regulatory risk, quality issues, or failure mode indicators from Section 1b:

- 100% of interactions where tool call failed (high signal)
- 100% of interactions with user feedback score ≤ 2 (quality issues)
- **100% of interactions containing GST/HSN references** (regulatory compliance — see CGST Act Section 73/74)
- **100% of interactions with online guardrail intervention** (regulatory or safety flag triggered)
- **100% of cash flow forecast interactions** (retention & milestone accuracy — RERA + Companies Act relevance)
- **100% of weekly summary outputs** (periodic deliverable with regulatory implications)
- 50% of interactions from new users (first 10 queries — ramp-up quality monitoring)
- 20% of interactions exceeding 30s response time (performance anomaly indicator)
- 20% of interactions where the model was routed to gpt-4o (complex query — higher hallucination surface area)
- 10% random sample of successful interactions (baseline quality)
- **All interactions sampled above are tagged in Langfuse with metadata:** query_type, domain_failure_modes_triggered (mapped to Section 1b failure mode numbers), regulatory_flags, model_used, tool_call_count

**Compliance Audit Trail:**

Langfuse traces serve as an audit trail for regulatory compliance (IT Act Section 44AA — 6-year record-keeping requirement):

- All traces must include: timestamp (IST), user role, query, full agent reasoning trace, tool call inputs and outputs, final response
- Langfuse database backed up daily to India-hosted object storage (AWS S3 Mumbai or equivalent)
- Retention: 6 years minimum (per IT Act Section 44AA read with Rule 6F)
- Monthly compliance report generated: count of traces, guardrail interventions by type, GST/HSN accuracy score, retention error rate, LLM judge calibration score
- Quarterly audit package: export of Langfuse traces for a representative week + calibration report + guardrail effectiveness summary, packaged for Compliance Officer review
